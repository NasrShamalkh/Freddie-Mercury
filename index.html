Freddie Mercury

One of the most talented and innovative singers of the rock era

Freddie Mercury's life over the years

  • 1946- Freddie Mercury was born Bulsara in Stone Town, Zanzibar (Tanzania).
  • 1953- Freddie began taking piano lessons.
  • 1964- The Zanzibar Revolution forced Freddie and his family to leave Zanzibar. They fled to England where they lived in Feltham. Freddie became an art student at Isleworth Polytechnic (West Thames College). After that he studied art and design (1966).
  • 1968- Freddie became friends with art student Tim Staffell who, along with astonomy student Brian May had formed the group Smile.
  • 1970- When Freddie’s friend, Tim Staffell, left the group Smile, Freddie Bulsara persuaded Brian May and group drummer Roger Taylor that he should replace Staffell as lead singer.and changed the band name to Queen.
  • 1971- John Deacon became the new bass player for Queen and Freddie changed his name into Freddie mercury.
  • 1973- Queen released their first album entitled ‘Queen’. It reached number 83 in the US and number 47 in the UK.
  • 1974- Queen released their second album Queen II. It reached number 5 in the UK charts and number 49 in the US. The group began a tour of America as supporting act to Mott The Hoople.
  • 1975- Freddie Mercury received an Ivor Novello award for his song ‘Killer Queen’. Also, The group wanted to release ‘Bohemian Rhapsody’, written by Freddie, as a single but they were told that at 5 minutes and 55 seconds no radio station would play it and it would be a flop. Freddie Mercury gave a copy of the single to his friend, DJ Kenny Everett, telling him he was not to play it but Everett did play it and other radio stations followed suit. After that, Queen released the single ‘Bohemian Rhapsody’. It stayed at number 1 in the UK charts for 9 consecutive weeks and sold over a million copies."it is now known as one of the greatest hits in the history of music".
  • 1976- Freddie ended his relationship with Mary Austin and began a relationship with a male employee at Elektra Records. He remained friends with Austin for the rest of his life. Freddie Mercury received an Ivor Novello Award for ‘Bohemian Rhapsody’.
  • (1978-1984)- During this period Queen released a number of world-wide hits and known to be as some of the best works in the music industry, these hits include:
    • "Bicycle ride" reached number 11 in UK.
    • "Dont stop me now" reached number 9 in UK.
    • "Live killers" reached number 3 in UK.
    • "Crazy litle things called love" reached number 2 in UK and number 1 in USA.
    • "Save me" reached number 11 in UK.
    • "Play the game" reached number 14 in UK.
    • The album "the game" reached number 1 in UK.
    • "Another one bites the dust" reached number 1 in USA.
    • Compilation album "Greatest hits" reached number 1 in UK and number 11 in USA.
    • "Radio Ga Ga" reached number 2 in UK.
  • 1985- Freddie began a relationship with a hairdresser called Jim Huton.
    Queen performed some of their greatest hits at live aid concert at wembley stadium. Their performance has been voted one of the greatest live rock music performances of all time.
  • 1987- Freddie Mercury was diagnosed with AIDS. The diagnosis was kept from the public.
  • 1990- Queen were awarded for their "outstanding contribution to British music".
  • 1991- Freddie retired of public life to his home in Kingiston. He was cared for by Jim Hutton and his life-long friend May Austin and released "These are the days of our lives" which reached number 1 in UK.
    In that year Freddie publicly anounced that he has Aids. One day after, he died of bronchial pneumonia which he had contracted as a complication of Aids.
